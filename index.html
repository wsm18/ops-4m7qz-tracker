            FM — Training

            AFT field manual
            Built to max your AFT (Deadlift · Hand-Release Push-up · Sprint-Drag-Carry · Plank · 2-Mile Run) and rebuild strength + endurance after your break. Runs on top of ROTC PT: on weeks you have PT, count it as one session and do the rest. Aim for 4 sessions/week, never two hard days back-to-back.
            🌱 Lean & bodyweight focus. This plan builds relative strength and lean muscle — the kind that helps your run and push-up scores, not bulk that slows them. The trick: get stronger by making bodyweight moves harder (tougher leverage, slower tempo, more reps, less rest), not by adding mass. External load stays minimal and only where the AFT demands it (deadlift + carries).
            🎯 Your priorities (from your AFT history): Your deadlift is your lowest event (70 pts, stuck at 200 lbs) and the closest to the 60-pt floor — it's your standing risk, so load it up. Your Sprint-Drag-Carry has slipped four tests running (79→74→69→65) — attack it with the AFT circuit. Push-ups, plank, and run are solid; maintain them. Banking those two weak events is your path from ~385 to 450+.
            Session 1 Strength A · Lower + Push

            Targets: Lower-body strength, Hand-Release Push-up · bodyweight-first

            Primary (bodyweight):
            • Bulgarian split squat — 4×10/leg (slow 3-sec lower; harder than it looks)
            • Single-leg Romanian deadlift — 3×10/leg (balance + posterior chain)
            • Hand-release push-ups — 4× max, 75s rest (count total reps, beat it weekly)
            • Pike push-ups → progress toward pseudo-planche — 3×8
            • Pistol squat progression (box/assisted) — 3×5/leg
            • Hollow-body hold — 3×30s
            If you have equipment (AFT deadlift prep — keep it lean):
            • Trap-bar / barbell deadlift — 4×6 moderate, crisp reps (strength, not max bulk)
            • Then do the bodyweight block above for volume
            Session 2 Conditioning · Run

            Targets: 2-Mile Run (your weak spot — this is the priority)

            Alternate each week between:
            • Intervals: warm up 5 min → 6× 400m hard / 90s walk → cool down. (Builds speed.)
            • Tempo: 1.5–2 mi at a "comfortably hard" pace you can barely talk through.
            • Long easy: 3–4 mi conversational pace. Builds the aerobic base that makes 2 miles feel short.

            Bonus: running keeps you lean and directly trains the event. Every 2–3 weeks, run a timed 2-mile and log it as a quest.

            Session 3 Strength B · Upper + Core

            Targets: Hand-Release Push-up, Pulling strength, Plank · bodyweight-first

            Primary (bodyweight):
            • Pull-ups or inverted rows (under a table/bar) — 4×8 (the missing piece of most BW plans)
            • Decline / pike push-ups — 4×10
            • Diamond push-ups — 3×12 (triceps + chest endurance)
            • Plank — 3× max hold, train past your AFT time
            • Side plank — 3×30s/side
            • Superman / back extensions — 3×15
            AFT carry prep (minimal load):
            • Backpack/ruck or farmer's carry — 4× 40m (trains Sprint-Drag-Carry grip + core)
            Session 4 AFT Circuit · Test Sim

            The "group PT" energy — race the clock, simulate the real test

            Do 3–4 rounds, rest 2 min between. Time yourself and try to beat it next week:

            • Sprint-Drag-Carry sim: 50m sprint → 50m backward drag (or bear crawl) → 50m loaded carry → 50m sprint
            • 20 hand-release push-ups
            • 15 squat jumps
            • 30–60s plank
            • 200m run

            This is the one to lean on when motivation's low — it's fast, competitive against yourself, and feels like a mission.

            📈 Progression (the bodyweight way): when a movement gets easy, don't add weight — make it harder. More reps, slower tempo, less rest, or a tougher variation (e.g. push-up → decline → pike → pseudo-planche). That's how you build lean strength without bulk. If a session feels easy, level it up. If you're wrecked, take the rest day — recovery is training.
            How to use this with the app: Each morning, your "Today's training session" daily points here — just do whichever session is next in the rotation. Check it off to earn XP, build your streak, and chip away at the AFT objective. The streak is your accountability when there's no one running PT next to you.
